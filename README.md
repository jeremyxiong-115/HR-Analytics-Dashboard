## ⚙️ How to Interact with the Dashboard
- Select different **education levels** to compare attrition patterns.
- Adjust **age bins** to explore turnover across different career stages.
- Hover over charts to view detailed metrics.
- Click on categories to filter and drill down into specific employee groups.

## 📌 Objective
The goal of this project is to analyze employee data and identify key factors driving employee attrition. The dashboard helps HR teams understand workforce patterns and make data-driven decisions to improve retention.

## 📊 Dataset
- Dataset: HR Analytics dataset
- Features include:
  - Employee demographics (age, gender, education)
  - Job-related factors (department, role, salary, overtime)
  - Performance and satisfaction metrics
- The data was cleaned and prepared before visualization.

## 🛠 Tools Used
- Tableau (data visualization & dashboard design)
- Excel / CSV (data preprocessing)

## 📈 Dashboard Overview
The dashboard provides an interactive view of:
- Overall employee attrition rate
- Attrition by department, job role, and demographics
- Impact of factors such as overtime, income, and job satisfaction
- Key trends and comparisons across different employee groups

## ⚙️ Interactive Features
- Dynamic filtering by department, role, education level, and age bin.
- Cross-filtering between charts for deeper exploration.
- Hover tooltips for detailed employee metrics.
- Drill-down capability to focus on high-risk groups.
- Helps HR teams identify patterns and target interventions.

## 🔍 Key Insights
- Employees who work overtime have a significantly higher attrition rate
- Younger employees show higher turnover compared to senior employees
- Certain departments (e.g., sales or high-pressure roles) experience more attrition
- Lower job satisfaction is strongly associated with employees leaving the company

## 💡 Recommendations
- Reduce excessive overtime to improve employee retention
- Focus on early-career employee engagement and support
- Improve job satisfaction through better management and work environment
- Allocate HR resources to high-attrition departments
